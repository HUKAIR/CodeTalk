# Separate evidence, interpretation, and judgment

Every potential decision conflict is presented as a decision review card that
keeps verifiable source evidence, optional generated interpretation, and the
maintainer's judgment visibly separate. The maintainer resolves the card as a
confirmed conflict, intentional exception, unrelated evidence, or insufficient
evidence; only a confirmed conflict that changes the proposed action counts as a
verified interception. This prevents readable model output from being mistaken
for proof while making raw history usable enough for a human decision.
